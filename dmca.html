DMCA and Copyright Requests

Contact: hello@brushjjaemu.space

Brush Jjaemu Space takes copyright and ownership concerns seriously. If you believe material on this site infringes your copyright or misuses your protected work, send a notice to hello@brushjjaemu.space so the request can be reviewed.

How to submit a copyright notice

To help the site evaluate a request efficiently, include enough detail to identify the work, the page, and the nature of the claim. In most cases, a useful notice should include the following:

  • Your full name and contact information.
  • A description of the copyrighted work you claim has been infringed.
  • The exact URL or URLs on Brush Jjaemu Space that you want reviewed.
  • A statement that you have a good-faith belief the disputed use is not authorized.
  • A statement that the information in your notice is accurate.
  • A statement that you are the owner or are authorized to act on behalf of the owner.

What happens after a notice is received

After a notice is received, Brush Jjaemu Space may review the claim, request more detail, temporarily limit access to a page, correct attribution, or remove disputed material while the matter is evaluated. The site may also keep records of notices to help address repeat issues or later correspondence.

Counter-notice information

If you believe material was removed or restricted by mistake, you may respond with a counter-notice that includes your contact information, the affected URL, an explanation of why you believe the action was taken in error, and a statement made in good faith. Brush Jjaemu Space may request additional clarification before restoring any disputed material.

Repeat issues and abusive notices

The site may refuse abusive, bad-faith, or clearly incomplete notices. It may also remove or limit access to material more quickly when repeated ownership complaints involve the same page or asset.

No legal advice

This page is provided for operational clarity and does not constitute legal advice. If you need legal guidance about copyright rights or formal takedown procedures, consult qualified counsel.
